World Happiness 2019

The world happiness report was created as an effort by the United Nations to give importance to happiness and wellbeing. Most of it's data are provided by the Gallup Global Emotions report which creates a happiness score ("Happiness Ladder") within a range of 0 to 10, with 10 being the happiest based on economic and social variables. The world map shows the Happiness score of 156 countries in 2019.

Is GDP a good measure for Happiness?

One of the major economic variables associated with the Happiness score is GDP per capita, It is measured by dividing the economic output of a country by it's population. This diagram shows how the happiness score is correlated with GDP per capita. A we can see, countries with a low GDP has a low score and countries with a high GDP has a higher happiness score.

Propertion of Happy people in the world over time

Happiness is simply, a feeling. Gallup's data measures multiple levels of wellbeing, gathering the emotional state of people around the world on a daily basis. It captures emotions as Enjoyment, Anger, Stress and level of life statisfaction of individuals. Learn about what proportion of people claimed to be happy in each country, over 30 years.
