World Happiness 2019

The world happiness report was created as an effort by the United Nations to give importance to happiness and wellbeing. Most of it's data are provided by the Gallup Global Emotions report which creates a happiness score ("Happiness Ladder") within a range of 0 to 10, with 10 being the happiest based on economic and social variables. The world map shows the Happiness score of 156 countries in 2019.

World Happiness and GDP

One of the major economic variables associated with the Happiness score is GDP per capita, It is measured by dividing the economic output of a country by it's population. This diagram shows how the happiness score is correlated with GDP per capita. A we can see, countries with a low GDP has a low score and countries with a high GDP has a higher happiness score. Isolate and explore data for each continent by clicking the continet in the legend.

Happiness Variables

As mentioned above, GDP per capita is one of the variables used to measure happiness. Some of the other variables are, Social support, Life expectancy and freedom to make life choices. All of these economic and social variables are related to each other, creating a certain level of inter dependency. The scatter plot matrix gives an overview how these variables were correlated in 2019. For example, Social support in European countries are higher compared to African countries, therefore we can conclude that there is a positive relationship between Social support and average life expectancy. Click on any continent in the legend to isolate data and explore the continet.

Proportion of Happy people in the world over time

Happiness is simply, a feeling. Gallup's data measures multiple levels of wellbeing, gathering the emotional state of people around the world on a daily basis. It captures emotions as Enjoyment, Anger, Stress and level of life statisfaction of individuals. Happiness data gathered over the years captures major events in history; both good and bad. For example, we can see how the happiness proportion has drastically declined in Egypt from 2009 to 2014, from my own research I found that the cause for this could be the Egyptian revolution of 2011 and incidents related to it.
